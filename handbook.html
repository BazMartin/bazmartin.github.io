Welcome to Los Santos City Services #

Your journey as a City Operative begins here.

Los Santos City Services is the government department responsible for Parking Enforcement and Public Works. This includes ticketing & impounding of vehicles in breach of violations, street maintenance and much more. As a City Operative, you represent the department with professionalism, integrity and dedicated to public service.

Mission Statement

To maintain order, safety, and functionality across Los Santos through dedicated service and community partnership.

Core Objective

As a department, our core objective is simple. To deliver a safe, clean city for the people that live in it. Whether it be through removing illegally parked cars, or street cleaning.

Your Impact

You make a difference. Whether it be cleaning a street, removing a vehicle, or repairing a street light, you improve Los Santos for our citizens and visitors.

This document forms the official handbook for City Operatives. Here, you can find information relating to every area of the department, including your role as a City Operative and what we have available in order for you to complete your job. If you are reading this handbook as a new operative to the department, you will find the Official Contact on the last page, which needs to be signed and submitted in full.

Internal Rules & Requirements #

Standards of conduct for all personnel.

Departmental Rules

  • Uniform: All operatives must wear full uniform while on duty. This relates to the uniforms offered in your lockers.
  • Vehicles: No use of personal vehicles while on duty. No speeding or dangerous driving in work vehicles.
  • Conduct: No smoking while in uniform. No transporting of individuals outside of the department using faction vehicles.
  • Facility Security: Ensure the gates on our sites are closed after use.
  • Personal Conduct: No inter-personnel relationships of any kind.
  • Confidentiality: No sharing of departmental information or data with ANY third parties.

Expectations

  • Professionalism: Always conduct yourself with respect toward citizens and fellow operatives. Represent with dignity.
  • Integrity: Honesty and ethical behavior are non-negotiable. Uphold the values of the department in every action.
  • Activity: Maintain regular attendance and active participation in departmental duties. Log hours via MDT Clock In/Out.
  • Chain of Command: Follow rank structure; escalate issues through proper channels. Respect seniority and protocol.
  • Equipment Care: Department vehicles and tools must be maintained and returned in good condition. Report any damage.
  • MDT Usage: Mobile Data Terminal is for official use only — no unauthorized activity. All reports must be accurate.
  • Reports: All enforcement actions, impounds, and public works tasks require detailed, factual MDT reports.

What Is A City Operative? #

Role definition and responsibilities.

A City Operative is a uniformed civilian employee responsible for maintaining public infrastructure, enforcing parking regulations, and supporting emergency services. Operatives work independently or in teams to ensure Los Santos remains safe, clean, and functional.

Key Responsibilities

  • Parking enforcement & citations
  • Vehicle impound authorization
  • Public works & maintenance
  • Incident documentation
  • Inter-agency coordination

In your role as a City Operative, you are responsible for the sectors Los Santos City Services offer. Whether it be removing vehicles parked in violation, or cleaning streets as and when requested. As a government department, you represent the Los Santos Government and should work accordingly, with pride.

As a City Operative, you work as part of a wider team in order to meet both our mission statement and requirements. This is done through attention to detail, sound judgement, professional communication and conflict management.

MDT System #

Mobile Data Terminal operations.

Your primary tool for reports, clock-in/out, and communication. All actions are logged and transmitted to department records.

Access

bazmartin.github.io

Reporting Guidelines

Complete Every Action

Every ticket, impound, public works task, and departmental action requires a corresponding MDT report.

Accuracy is Critical

Reports must be factual, detailed, and submitted in real-time. Double-check all information before transmitting.

Evidence Required

Always attach clear image evidence (screenshots, photos) to support your reports. No exceptions.

Real-Time Submission

Reports should be submitted immediately after completing the action, not at the end of shift.

Duty Logging

  • Clock In: Select "Duty Log" tab and click "Clock In" at the start of your shift.
  • Clock Out: Select "Duty Log" tab and click "Clock Out" at the end of your shift.
  • Attendance Tracking: All clock-in/out events are logged and recorded for attendance monitoring.
  • Missed Logs: Failure to clock in/out may result in unreported absences being flagged for review.

Pro Tip

Bookmark the MDT link on your device. Log in at the start of every shift and keep the tab open for quick report submissions throughout your duty.

LSCS - Impounding

As a City Operative, you are required to complete shifts for the Parking Enforcement Division. Part of this work includes the impounding of vehicles that fail to meet our guidelines.

In order to complete the impounding of a vehicle in a safe manner, you must follow the below protocol:

  • Identify a vehicle that fails to meet our guidelines.
  • Check the vehicle license plate using PMC.
  • Use /dl and take a clear screenshot of the vehicle offending.
  • Hook the vehicle with a tow truck.
  • Transport the vehicle back to the Impound Yard on Azalea Street.
  • Set the vehicle down in a center parking bay.
  • /impound the vehicle
  • Complete an Action Report using the Impound Report option, under parking enforcement.

LSCS - Ticketing

As a City Operative, you are required to complete shifts for the Parking Enforcement Division. Part of this work includes the ticketing of vehicles that fail to meet our guidelines.

In order to complete the ticketing of a vehicle in a safe manner, you must follow the below protocol:

  • Identify a vehicle that doesn't meet the parking guidelines.
  • Check the vehicle on /pmc for previous tickets on the same incident, repeat offending.
  • Providing the vehicle hasn't been ticketed for the same issue, or the vehicle isn't a repeat offender, proceed with ticketing the vehicle.
  • Stand near the vehicle, and use /vticket.
  • Complete the details as the command requires.
  • Issue the ticket.
  • Take a screenshot of the vehicle using /dl
  • Complete an action report for a Vehicle Ticket.

Important

Use of /vticket and /impound are monitored, and reports are regularly audited. Ensure all tickets and impounds are legitimate and properly documented.

LSCS - Road Sweeping

As a City Operative, you are required to complete shifts for the Public Works Division. This includes Road Sweeping.

In order to conduct Road Sweeping in a safe manner, you must follow the below protocol:

  • Prepare a Road Sweeper from the Idlewood Depot.
  • Conduct a walkaround safety check.
  • Pre-plan your overall route to include high-traffic areas.
  • Leave the depot and join the road.
  • Stay to the side of the road against the sidewalk, to ensure the road is clean.
  • Drive at less than 25 miles per hour.
  • Complete your route in full, ideally both sides.
  • Complete an Action Report under Public Works.

LSCS - Traffic Management

Traffic Management operations ensure safe passage through construction zones, events, or accident scenes.

In order to conduct Traffic Management in a safe manner, you must follow the below protocol:

  • Assess the area requiring traffic management.
  • Deploy traffic cones and barriers to create a safe work zone.
  • Place appropriate warning signs (road work, lane closure, etc.).
  • Wear full high-visibility uniform and safety gear.
  • Direct traffic using hand signals or flags when necessary.
  • Coordinate with team members via radio for safe flow.
  • Document the setup with clear photographic evidence.
  • Complete an Action Report under Public Works with the 4-digit Task Reference Number.

LSCS - Street Light Repairs

Maintaining street lighting ensures public safety and visibility throughout Los Santos.

In order to conduct Street Light Repairs in a safe manner, you must follow the below protocol:

  • Identify faulty street lights during patrol or via citizen reports.
  • Record the location (street name, nearest intersection, pole number if available).
  • Prepare the appropriate vehicle with repair equipment (Cherry Picker or Utility Truck).
  • Set up traffic cones and warning signs around the work area.
  • Inspect the light (bulb, wiring, electrical components).
  • Replace faulty bulbs or components as needed.
  • Test the light to ensure functionality.
  • Document before and after photos of the repair.
  • Complete an Action Report under Public Works with the 4-digit Task Reference Number.

LSCS - Traffic Light Repairs

Traffic signal maintenance is critical for road safety and traffic flow.

In order to conduct Traffic Light Repairs in a safe manner, you must follow the below protocol:

  • Identify faulty traffic lights (stuck red, flashing, non-operational, incorrect timing).
  • Record the intersection location and nature of the fault.
  • Deploy appropriate vehicle with traffic signal repair equipment.
  • Set up comprehensive traffic management around the intersection.
  • If required, coordinate with team members for manual traffic direction.
  • Access the signal control cabinet and diagnose the issue.
  • Perform necessary repairs (timing adjustment, bulb replacement, wiring fix).
  • Test the signal through all phases to ensure proper operation.
  • Document the repair with before and after evidence.
  • Complete an Action Report under Public Works with the 4-digit Task Reference Number.

Important Note

All Public Works tasks require a 4-digit Task Reference Number. This number should be recorded in your Action Report for tracking and auditing purposes. Always ensure your reports include clear before and after evidence.

Abandoned vehicles, repeat offenders, or vehicles blocking emergency access require immediate impound action. Impound orders require photo evidence and guideline selection.

In order to complete the impounding of a vehicle in a safe manner, you must follow the below protocol:

  • Identify: Identify a vehicle that fails to meet our guidelines (abandoned, blocking access, repeat offender, etc.).
  • Check License Plate: Check the vehicle license plate using PMC.
  • Document the Offense: Use /dl and take a clear screenshot of the vehicle offending.
  • Hook the Vehicle: Hook the vehicle with a tow truck.
  • Transport: Transport the vehicle back to the Impound Yard on Azalea Street, Commerce.
  • Position the Vehicle: Set the vehicle down in a center parking bay within the impound yard.
  • Issue Impound: Use /impound to officially impound the vehicle.
  • Complete Report: Complete an Action Report using the Vehicle Impound option, under parking enforcement.

LSPD / FD / Other Agencies #

Inter-departmental coordination.

LSCS works closely with other government agencies to support city operations. When responding to requests from LSPD, LSFD, or other departments, proper protocol ensures safety and effective collaboration.

Request Types

Vehicle Removal

Removing vehicles from crime scenes, accidents, or blocked areas.

Scene Cleanup

Clearing debris, hazards, or spillages after incidents.

Traffic Management

Assisting with road closures, diversions, and traffic control.

Infrastructure Support

Repairing damaged street furniture, barriers, or utilities.

Response Protocol

When responding to an external agency request, follow these procedures to ensure safety and professionalism:

  • Acknowledge the Request: Confirm receipt of the request via radio or MDT. Note the requesting officer's name, agency, and location.
  • Obtain Officer Details: Record the requesting officer's full name, badge number, and agency. This must be included in your MDT report.
  • Full Uniform Required: Wear full LSCS uniform with high-visibility vest. Identify yourself clearly upon arrival.
  • Scene Assessment: Upon arrival, assess the scene. Identify potential hazards, active police activity, and the safest approach.
  • Check In With Command: Report to the senior officer on scene. Introduce yourself and confirm what assistance is required.
  • Document Everything: Take clear photographs of the scene, the work area, and the completed task. Use /dl for evidence.
  • Secure Your Workspace: Use cones, barriers, or flares as needed to create a safe work zone. Never work inside an active crime scene without authorization.
  • Remain Aware: Stay alert to ongoing police activity. Do not interfere with investigations. If instructed to wait, comply and remain at a safe distance.
  • Complete the Task: Perform the requested service efficiently and safely. Communicate progress to the on-scene commander.
  • Submit MDT Report: Complete an External Request report via the MDT. Include requesting officer details, location, service provided, and evidence images.

Scene Safety Guidelines

  • Stay Clear of Active Crime Scenes: Do not enter cordoned areas unless explicitly authorized by a police supervisor.
  • Maintain Radio Contact: Stay on the designated frequency and monitor for updates or changes to the situation.
  • Remain Visible: Wear high-visibility gear. Make your presence known to officers to avoid being mistaken for a threat.
  • Know Your Exit: Always be aware of the safest exit route in case the situation escalates.
  • Wait for Clearance: If instructed to wait before entering or beginning work, do not proceed until given explicit clearance.
  • Follow Instructions: Officers on scene have authority over safety. Follow their directions without question.
  • Secure Equipment: Ensure all tools and vehicles are safely positioned away from traffic and active incident zones.

MDT Reporting Requirements

All external agency responses must be documented through the External Request tab in MDT. Include the following:

  • Report Code: Auto-generated unique identifier (RQ prefix).
  • Date & Time: When the request was received and completed.
  • Requesting Department: LSPD, LSFD, LSGOV, BI, or Other.
  • Requesting Officer: Full name, badge number, and agency.
  • Location: Exact address or intersection of the incident.
  • Service Requested: Detailed description of the assistance provided.
  • Evidence: Clear images showing before, during, and after the work.
  • LSCS Operative: Your name and badge number.

Professional Conduct

When working alongside other agencies, you represent LSCS. Maintain professionalism, respect scene authority, and coordinate effectively. Positive inter-agency relationships are essential for a safe and functional city.
